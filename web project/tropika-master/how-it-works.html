Instructions

1. Donate Fresh Fruits and Vegetables:
- Encourage users to donate fresh fruits and vegetables that are still in good condition, promoting a healthy and diverse food supply.

2. Avoid Perishable Items Close to Expiry:
- Discourage the donation of perishable items with a short shelf life, especially if they are close to their expiration date to ensure the safety and quality of donated food.

3. Donate Non-Perishable Staples:
- Recommend non-perishable staples such as canned goods, dry grains, and pasta, which have a longer shelf life and can be stored easily.

4. Avoid Damaged or Opened Packages:
- Instruct donors not to contribute items with damaged or opened packaging to maintain food safety standards.

5. Donate Unopened and Non-Expired Items:
- Stress the importance of donating unopened items with intact packaging and a reasonable expiration date to ensure the health and safety of recipients.

6. Avoid Homemade or Unlabeled Foods:
- Advise against donating homemade or unlabeled items to comply with food safety regulations and provide clear information about ingredients.

7. Donate Nutrient-Dense Foods:
- Encourage the donation of nutrient-dense foods that contribute to a balanced diet, such as whole grains, lean proteins, and low-sugar options.

8. Avoid Foods with Strong Odors:
- Discourage the donation of foods with strong odors to maintain a pleasant and hygienic environment for recipients.

9. Donate Allergen-Free or Clearly Labeled Items:
- Suggest donating items that are allergen-free or have clear labeling of potential allergens to ensure the safety of individuals with dietary restrictions.

10. Avoid Expired or Recalled Products:
- Emphasize the importance of checking for product recalls and avoiding the donation of items that may have been recalled or are past their expiration date.
